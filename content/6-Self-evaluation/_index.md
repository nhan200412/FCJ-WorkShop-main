# Internship Self-Assessment

After completing the 12-week AWS Cloud Workshop, I had the opportunity to learn and practice many core AWS services through hands-on labs and a team project. This internship helped me strengthen both my cloud computing knowledge and practical skills.

---

### 1. Work Completion

I would evaluate my overall work completion at approximately **90%**.

* Successfully completed all weekly AWS hands-on labs.
* Participated in developing and presenting the Serverless Backend project on AWS.
* Completed weekly reports and assigned tasks on schedule.

However, there is still room for improvement. The frontend development was sometimes delayed compared to the backend, and some advanced features, such as Redis caching, were not implemented due to time constraints.

---

### 2. Skills Acquired

**Technical Skills:**

* Gained practical experience with AWS services such as Amazon VPC, EC2, IAM, S3, RDS, CloudWatch, and Lambda.
* Learned how to deploy, manage, and monitor cloud infrastructure on AWS.
* Explored AI/ML services and serverless architecture on AWS.

**Soft Skills:**

* Improved my ability to read and understand technical documentation in English.
* Enhanced my teamwork, problem-solving, and time management skills.

---

### 3. Strengths and Areas for Improvement

**Strengths:**

* Proactively explored new technologies and completed all required labs.
* Maintained a positive learning attitude and collaborated effectively with teammates during the project.

**Areas for Improvement:**

* Continue learning more about AI/ML services and optimizing serverless architectures.
* Gain more experience in deploying and managing large-scale real-world cloud applications.

---

### 4. Future Plans

In the future, I plan to continue studying advanced AWS services, prepare for an AWS certification, and apply the knowledge I have gained to real-world cloud projects. In addition, I plan to further enhance the HMN Bakery project by integrating Redis to reduce the workload on DynamoDB and improve overall application performance.
